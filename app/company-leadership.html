The leadership

EXECUTIVES

Adriaan van Wyk

CEO

Adriaan is a co-founder of SourceCode Technology Holdings, Inc. He assumed his current position upon inception in 1997. Adriaan has a degree in accounting and a post-graduate degree in information systems (computer science). He likes to bring together his two loves: technology and business.

When not at work, he can be found indulging in sports -- cycling, running, swimming, mountain climbing -- or listening to music, reading and, most importantly, spending time with his kids.

Warren Bingham

COO and CFO

As COO, Warren is responsible for global consulting services, technical support, sales support, and operations.

As CFO, Warren oversees global accounting and finance, legal affairs, human resources, and management information systems.

Before joining K2, Warren worked at Insightful Corporation and, prior to that, Concur Technologies, both publicly traded software companies. He was responsible for all global accounting operations at the two companies, as well as public reporting and disclosure requirements. Warren also served as an auditor for PricewaterhouseCoopers, and then as corporate controller for several VC-backed software and internet companies.

Warren graduated from Washington State University with degrees in accounting and finance. Warren also served in the U.S. Navy as a nuclear-reactor operator, onboard a U.S. Trident submarine.

Olaf Wagner

SVP, Product Management

Olaf is a co-founder of SourceCode Technology Holdings, Inc. He assumed his current position in 1997.

Olaf is responsible for the Product Planning and Roadmap for the entire K2 product family. Olaf has a bachelor of science (honors) degree in computer science.

Koos du Preez

CTO

Koos has the overall responsibility for the direction and architecture of the entire SourceCode product stack. He joined K2 in 2004 and assumed his current position in 2006.

Koos studied electronic engineering and has spent over 20 years in the IT sector. His deeply rooted passion for technology spans the entire spectrum — from designing and building hardware to developing firmware, device drivers and application software. Prior to joining SourceCode, he held the position of chief software architect at a California-based High Availability software vendor.

Dave Marcus

SVP, Technology Partners

Dave is responsible for the K2 partner program, technology alliances (particularly with Microsoft) , and for driving business development with current and new K2 partners.

Before joining K2, he worked at Microsoft for 14 years, eight of them with Microsoft Southern Africa in technical sales and sales management positions. He then moved to Microsoft corporate headquarters in the U.S., primarily focused on global field and partner sales enablement in a variety of roles.

Dave has an engineering degree from the University of Cape Town in South Africa.

Ingrid Rood

SVP, Americas Sales

Ingrid is based in Bellevue, Washington, and is responsible for K2 business in The Americas.

Ingrid has held numerous sales and operational roles in the technology sector. Prior to K2 , Ingrid was Vice President , Public Sector Sales at The Active Network and General Manager, State and Local Government Sales for Microsoft Corporation.

Steven Rees-Pullman

SVP, EMEA

Steven has held a number of sales and operational roles during 15 years in the Technology Sector across Europe, Middle East, Africa and Asia Pacific, most recently as the EMEA Vice President for SumTotal Systems.

He has managed to combine his loves of travel and business to gain extensive experience working internationally. He managed the Asia Pacific business for Torex and has a good knowledge of all sales delivery models having previously managed the indirect sales organizations for Infor and SSA as Vice President of Channels.

Outside of work Steven likes to be active and can be found running, riding and spending time with his wife and kids.

KOH Weng Him

SVP, Asia Pacific

Weng Him has overall P and L responsibility for SourceCode Asia Pacific business. His key role is to create a profitable product business; develop a highly skilled and motivated sales and support team; and create associated partnerships, business infrastructures and processes required to support the business.

Weng Him focuses on helping clients optimize the deployment of their invaluable resources to optimize business efficiency, reduce process execution cycle times and increase availability of process-related information. Weng Him joined SourceCode with more than 15 years of IT sales, marketing and consulting experience with leading organizations like Price Waterhouse, Accenture and IBM Global Services.

Weng Him has an MBA (with distinction) from the University of Hull, U.K., and a bachelor of accountancy from Nanyang Technological University, Singapore.

Glen Bryant

VP, Customer Account Management

Glen is responsible for global account management across the worldwide K2 business.

Over the past decade, he has held numerous roles with K2, spanning business development, sales and general management. His passions are to grow relationships and ensure the success of K2 customers.

Glen has a Bachelor of Science degree in mathematics and chemistry, and before venturing into the world of technology, he started his career as a high school science teacher. Glen resides in Denver, Colorado, and divides his time between his family, friends, food and fly-fishing in the Rocky Mountains.

Barry Hoffmann

VP, Worldwide Support

Barry has been an integral resource for K2’s customer service and technical support initiatives since 2003. Today he is ultimately responsible for the K2 support teams worldwide and for continuously refining the K2 customer experience.

“I love creating new and engaging solutions to enhance customer service and then implementing them across the globe,” he said.

Barry also enjoys developing technical teams and creating new opportunities for internal resources.

Rob Speck

VP, Worldwide Services

Rob currently leads K2’s global consulting services unit. During his tenure at K2, Rob has led the expansion of services operations, software enablement and delivery options for customers.

Prior to K2, Rob was a Principal at TIBCO Software where he managed large account services within global accounts. He also served as Vice President and Principal at Nimbus Partners as well as Director of Software Product Development at Neudesic. Rob founded and led AVIVA Consulting Group; merging with Neudesic in 2006. Rob has over twenty-five years of experience leading software development, marketing and sales efforts. He holds a BA in Economics and an MBA in Finance from Clark University. He began his career as a financial analyst and bond analyst, before moving to software technology; first with Lotus Development and later with Cambridge Technology Partners.

He resides in the desert southwest of the U.S. with his family and has passions for drumming, song-writing, photography, and baseball.

Christo van Heerden

VP, Engineering - South Africa

Christo joined SourceCode in 2002 and is responsible for the product development side of the business. He leads a dynamic team that develops, tests, documents and deploys the K2 products in sometimes-challenging customer environments.

Christo has a bachelor of commerce degree (informatics) from the University of Pretoria in South Africa.

Denise Parker

VP, Worldwide Channel Partners and Channel Sales

Denise Parker leads the Global Channel team overseeing the US, EMEA and APAC teams. K2’s channel engagement spans across many partner types, VARs, Service Providers, ISVs, Distributors and Global SIs. Prior to K2, Denise has held several leadership positions at Microsoft, leading the WW Alliance team in the Enterprise group and managing the Enterprise Partner team for the German market, to name a few.

She has lead business development and sales organizations in Asia, Europe and North American markets. Denise holds a Masters in Engineering and a PhD in Technology Management from the Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich, Switzerland.

Hubert Cheng

General Counsel

Hubert is the company’s chief, in-house legal officer.

Before joining K2, Hubert worked as counsel in the legal departments of Microsoft and Oracle, two of the world’s largest software companies. He has also worked for RealNetworks and was an associate at Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati, Silicon Valley’s premier technology law firm.

Hubert graduated from Stanford University with a B.S. in biology, and received his law degree from the University of California, Berkeley School of Law.

BOARD OF DIRECTORS

Brian Decker

Brian Decker is a Principal with Francisco Partners and focuses on investments in the software sector, with emphasis on security and infrastructure software. Prior to joining Francisco Partners in 2008, Brian was a management consultant with McKinsey & Company focusing on the technology sector. Earlier in his career, Brian held a variety of product and marketing roles at Mindspeed Technologies and Skyworks Solutions. Brian currently serves on the board of directors of Plex Systems and Healthland and previously served on the board of Grass Valley.

Brian holds a BASc in Honors Computer Engineering with Distinction from the University of Waterloo and an M.Eng. in Electrical Engineering from Cornell University.

David Golob

David Golob joined Francisco Partners as a Partner in 2001 and focuses primarily on investments in the software and services sectors. Prior to joining Francisco Partners, David was a Managing Director with Tiger Management, where he was co-head of the global technology group and focused in the software industry. David spent seven years as a private equity investor focused on the software industry at General Atlantic Partners and Sutter Hill Ventures. Earlier in his career, he was a management consultant with McKinsey & Company and a Product Manager with Newport Systems Solutions, Inc. (acquired by Cisco). David currently serves on the board of directors of Avangate, Barracuda Networks, Micro Focus, PayLease, and Prosper. He previously served on the boards of Aderant, Attachmate, City Index, Foundation 9, Grass Valley, GXS, Office Tiger, Primavera, and RedPrairie.

David holds an A.B. from Harvard College, summa cum laude, and an M.B.A. from Stanford Graduate School of Business.

Paul Weinstein

Paul Weinstein is a founding general partner at Azure Capital Partners, a San Francisco-based venture capital firm with more than $750 million under management. Prior to founding Azure, Paul was a managing director with Credit Suisse, Deutsche Bank, PaineWebber and Kidder Peabody. With more than 25 years of investment experience, Paul’s primary investment focus is on communications technology, data center, cloud infrastructure and applications and mobility solutions. Paul, chairman of Unitas, also represents Azure Capital on the Boards of Unitas, Fonality, InterModalData, SwitchFly, K2 Software and Load Dynamix. He is a graduate of Babson College with a B.S. degree in Finance and has earned the Chartered Financial Analyst designation.

Rudolf Pretorius

Rudolf Pretorius is the non executive director of SourceCode Technology Holdings, Inc. He assumed his current position in 2001.

Rudolf represents Treacle Private Equity, which provided the initial capital for SourceCode. Qualified as a chartered accountant, he worked for a South African investment bank for 12 years prior to establishing Treacle Private Equity in 2000.

A venture capitalist with a focus on backing entrepreneurial management teams that possess the ability to develop international-standard software in South Africa’s low-cost environment, Rudolf indulges in outdoor activities, which include jogging, mountain and road biking, and mountaineering, when not at work.

Adriaan van Wyk

Adriaan van Wyk is the CEO co-founder of SourceCode Technology Holdings, Inc. He assumed his current position upon inception in 1997. Adriaan has a degree in accounting and a post-graduate degree in information systems (computer science). He likes to bring together his two loves: technology and business.

When not at work, he can be found indulging in sports -- cycling, running, swimming, mountain climbing -- or listening to music, reading and, most importantly, spending time with his kids.

INVESTORS

Azure Capital Partners

Founded in 2000, Azure is a San Francisco-based venture capital firm with over $750 million under management. We invest in early stage technology companies that are at the forefront of a transformative opportunity for growth. We have served as trusted advisors to some of the most successful and important technology companies created in the last decade, including VMware (NYSE: VMW), Bill Me Later (acquired by eBay), Calix (NYSE: CALX), Top Tier (acquired by SAP) and World Wide Packets (acquired by Ciena), and have helped to generate billions of dollars of value in these companies for our entrepreneurs and investors. The Azure team is recognized for industry thought leadership, a broad network of powerful industry relationships, and a unique professional investment approach to venture investing. We passionately support our portfolio companies with active guidance in all key aspects of team building, strategy, operations and governance.

For more information, please visit www.azurecap.com

Francisco

Francisco Partners is a global private equity firm that specializes in investments in technology companies. Since its launch fifteen years ago, FP has raised approximately $10 billion and invested in more than 150 technology companies, making it one of the most active investors in the industry. The firm invests in transaction values ranging from $50 million to over $2 billion, where the firm’s deep sub-sector knowledge and operational expertise can help a company realize its full potential.

For more information, please visit www.franciscopartners.com.

FTV Capital

FTV Capital provides growth capital to companies seeking to finance organic expansion, recapitalizations, build-ups and buyouts. The firm invests in software and business services companies that derive value from its unmatched Global Partner Network, which includes many of the world’s leading financial institutions. FTV Capital’s Global Partner Network provides the firm with a unique vantage point into the business-driven IT and operating challenges of the global enterprise. Founded in 1998, FTV Capital currently has more than $1 billion under management and offices in San Francisco and New York.
